The blog is a further research about COVID-19 in Belgium. According to Johns Hopkins University Center for System Science and Engineering invention provided latest version of the data, in the first 180 days Belgium reported death number took a small place in the European Union, however, Belgium took the highest death rate in the world which we found the result in the last blog. Figure 1 shows reported deaths number in 8 particular European countries in the first 180 days since the epidemic spread out. The graph shows the same trend were increased rapidly in the first 50 days.

Figure 1: Reported deaths number in European Union.

Thanks for lockdown policy in many countries that made the outbreak under control, in figure2 that the latest update in 14th Sep 2020, reported deaths number in European mostly were under 100 cases. In Belgium, it seems that outbreak is under control that keep the number under 10 cases.

Figure 2: Covid19:Reported deaths(new cases per day)

As it surveys an economy ravaged by the disease, figure 3 listed that Belgium real gross domestic product (GDP) from first quarter of 2005 to first quarter of 2020. There are two apparent fluctuations that one was from 2008 to 2009 which meet economic crisis, another one was from late 2019 to recent which under COVID-19 outbreak. The horizon line can clearly clarify that first quarter of 2020, GDP for Belgium fell to same level of 2019 which just went through an economic crisis.

Meanwhile, Belgium limps back to work after the outbreak, citizens job prospects are truly bleak. It was a period that the workforce as prospective employers mull lay-offs of hiring freezes. For a middle class used to relentlessly strong economic growth, the stock will be great. Figure 5 illustrated unemployment rate from first quarter of 2015 to first quarter of 2020, it seems that there was a tendency to decrease unemployment rate since 2015 before epidemic happened. The latest data showed that unemployment rate in Belgium has obvious rose in first quarter in 2020.
